PROPER LEVEL — HOW TO USE
=========================
Version 1.0  |  ca.themillrace.proper.level
Open source — MIT Licence  |  github.com/millrace/proper-level

──────────────────────────────────────────────
LEVEL MODE
──────────────────────────────────────────────
The app detects how you're holding your phone and automatically
switches between three level types. You don't need to pick one.

  HORIZONTAL (phone landscape)
  Hold your phone in landscape with one long edge resting on
  the surface you want to check — shelf, countertop, door frame.
  The bubble moves left and right. Center it for level.

  VERTICAL (phone portrait)
  Hold your phone in portrait with one edge against a wall,
  door frame, or stud. The bubble indicates plumb (true vertical).

  SURFACE 360° (phone flat)
  Set your phone flat on any surface. The bullseye shows level
  in all directions simultaneously. The dot should sit inside
  the innermost ring for level.

AUTO / LOCK
  The mode label at the top shows which sub-mode is active.
  Tap AUTO ▾ to manually lock to a specific mode — useful
  when you're holding the phone in an unusual angle to reach
  a tight spot.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
CLINOMETER
──────────────────────────────────────────────
Measures the angle of inclination of any surface.

  Hold your phone with one edge flat against the surface
  you want to measure. The readout shows the angle from
  horizontal in degrees (or % grade — toggle with the
  button at the bottom).

TARGET ANGLE
  Tap SET TARGET to enter a desired angle. The app will
  show the delta and alert you (vibration + audio) when
  you hit it. Useful for:
  • Stair risers (common angles: 34°–38°)
  • Roof pitches (common: 18°–34°)
  • Bevel cuts (any custom angle)
  • Drainage slopes (tile, gutter: 1–2%)

──────────────────────────────────────────────
FREEZE / HOLD
──────────────────────────────────────────────
Locks the current reading on screen so you can move the
phone away and read the number at your leisure.

  • Tap anywhere on the reading to toggle freeze
  • Tap the HOLD button in the top toolbar
  • Volume-up button also toggles freeze

A "HOLD" badge appears on the reading when frozen.
The mode sub-type is also locked while frozen.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
AUDIO & VIBRATION FEEDBACK
──────────────────────────────────────────────
The app guides you to level without needing to look at the screen.

  AUDIO: A tone rises in pitch as you approach level, then
  holds a steady note when level is achieved. Tempo increases
  as you get closer. Useful when your hands are occupied.

  VIBRATION: A pulse fires with increasing tempo as you approach
  level. A double-buzz confirms when level is achieved.

  Toggle both from the toolbar icons at the top of every screen.
  Tapping the audio icon ( 🔊 ) or vibration icon ( 📳 ) toggles
  each independently.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
FLASHLIGHT
──────────────────────────────────────────────
The flashlight icon ( 🔆 ) in the top toolbar toggles your
phone's rear torch — for working in dark cabinets, under sinks,
or in poorly lit spaces. It resets off every time the app launches.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
CALIBRATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────
Corrects for manufacturing variance in your phone's accelerometer.
Most phones are accurate enough without calibration, but if your
readings seem consistently off by a fixed amount, calibrate.

  Settings → Calibrate Now

STEP 1: Rest your phone flat on the most level surface available.
        Tap CAPTURE STEP 1.

STEP 2: Rotate the phone 180° end-for-end (spin it like a steering
        wheel) on the same surface. Tap CAPTURE STEP 2.

The two-step method cancels out any surface imperfection — you
don't need a perfectly level surface to get a good calibration.

RESET: Settings → Reset to factory removes the calibration offset
       and returns to raw sensor readings.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SETTINGS
──────────────────────────────────────────────
  Level threshold   How close to 0° triggers "LEVEL" state.
                    Default: 0.5°. Tighter = more precise.

  Sensitivity       How fast the bubble responds.
                    Fast = snappy, Smooth = damped.
                    Use Smooth in shaky environments.

  Angle units       Degrees or % Grade (slope percentage).

  Distance units    Metric or Imperial. Defaults to your locale.

  Audio / Vibration Toggle feedback channels independently.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
TIPS FROM THE FIELD
──────────────────────────────────────────────
• For the most accurate reading, wait 1–2 seconds after
  placing the phone before reading — the filter needs to settle.

• On a very long surface (e.g., checking a 10-foot countertop),
  take readings at both ends and the middle.

• Freeze is your best friend when working alone — set it before
  you pull the phone away so the reading stays visible.

• The threshold ring on the bullseye (Surface mode) corresponds
  to your current level threshold setting. Change it in Settings
  if you need tighter or looser tolerances.

• In Clinometer mode, hold the phone with the spine (long edge)
  flat against the surface for the most accurate angle reading.
